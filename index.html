What drew you towards Computer Science?

I've always enjoyed solving puzzles and figuring out how things work. Programming felt like the perfect combination of logic and creativity. The satisfaction of solving a difficult problem or building something useful from scratch is what keeps me coming back every day.

What do you enjoy most about coding?

I love the feeling of turning an idea into reality. Whether it's a website, a tool, or a small automation script, there's something satisfying about creating something from a blank screen. Debugging can be frustrating, but solving that one stubborn bug often feels like winning a mini battle.

What do you do when you're not coding?

You'll usually find me watching cricket, following Virat Kohli's latest innings, working out, exploring new technologies, or simply relaxing with music and good YouTube videos. Sometimes I spend hours researching random topics just because they seem interesting.

Is there someone who inspires you?

Virat Kohli has always been one of my biggest inspirations. Beyond the records and achievements, I admire his discipline, consistency, work ethic, and the way he constantly pushes himself to improve. His mindset reminds me that success is built through daily effort rather than occasional motivation.

What is your idea of a perfect day?

A productive coding session, a good gym workout, an exciting cricket match, some time spent learning something new, and ending the day knowing I'm slightly better than I was yesterday. Nothing extravagant—just steady progress and good vibes.
